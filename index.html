Author & Engineering Consulting

Work, duty, risk, and the way a life forms its code.

Erick L. Sokn grew up on a working farm in rural Illinois, later served as a Military Policeman in the United States Army, worked as a structural ironworker, and then spent decades as an engineer solving difficult manufacturing and product-development problems.

His writing draws directly from those worlds. The memoir and poetry center on responsibility, judgment under pressure, work done by hand, work done under risk, and the hard-earned code that carries from one life stage into the next.

His consulting work applies that same practical discipline to injection molding, manufacturing problem solving, design-for-manufacturing, and technical training.
