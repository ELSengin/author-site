About Erick L. Sokn

Erick L. Sokn grew up on a working farm in rural Illinois where work, responsibility, and consequence were part of daily life from the start. After high school he enlisted in the United States Army and served as a Military Policeman, working in environments where judgment had to be made quickly and carried real stakes.

After military service he worked in physically demanding trades, including structural ironwork, before moving into engineering. Over the course of his career he focused on difficult design, tooling, manufacturing, and product-development problems, often in situations where the margin for error was small and the timeline was short.

His writing draws on those experiences directly. The memoir and poems center on work, duty, risk, moral line, pressure, and the way one stage of life shapes the next. The emphasis is not on invention or performance. It is on lived experience, practical truth, and the code formed by work and consequence.
